Different Ways to Cook Potatoes

Mashed Potatoes

Prep time: 15 minutes

Cook time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.4 kg (3 pounds) russet potatoes, cut into large chunks
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 115 g butter, plus extra for serving
  • 250 ml whole milk
  • 1 ½ teaspoons sea salt, plus extra for the cooking water
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 60–120 ml sour cream (optional)
  • Fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)
Method
  1. Place the potatoes and garlic in a large pot and cover with cold water. Add a tablespoon of salt.
  2. Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes, until the potatoes are tender. Drain well and return to the pot.
  3. Mash the potatoes and garlic together. Add the butter, milk, salt and pepper, and mash until smooth. Fold through sour cream if using.
  4. Check seasoning, then serve topped with extra butter and fresh chives.

French Fries

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • Salt
  • 2 litres vegetable oil (for frying)
Method
  1. Cut the potatoes into chips and place in a saucepan with 2 litres of water, the vinegar, and 2 tablespoons of salt. Bring to the boil and cook for 10 minutes until tender but not falling apart. Drain and leave to dry on paper towels.
  2. Heat the oil in a deep pan to 200°C. Fry the chips in batches for about 1 minute, then remove and drain. Allow them to cool (you can freeze them at this stage for later).
  3. Reheat the oil to 200°C. Fry the chips again for 3–4 minutes until crisp and golden. Drain on paper towels, season with salt, and serve hot.

Roast Potatoes

Ingredients

  • 1 kg potatoes (your choice of variety)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: rosemary, garlic, or thyme
Method
  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Peel and cut the potatoes into chunks. Toss with olive oil, salt, pepper, and herbs if using.
  3. Spread evenly on a baking tray and roast for 40–50 minutes, turning halfway, until golden and crispy.
